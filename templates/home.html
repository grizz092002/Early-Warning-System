About Dengue Fever

Dengue fever is a mosquito-borne viral infection that causes flu-like symptoms, including high fever, severe headache, pain behind the eyes, joint and muscle pain, nausea, vomiting, and rash. In severe cases, it can lead to dengue hemorrhagic fever, characterized by bleeding, low platelet count, and organ damage. Dengue is prevalent in tropical and subtropical regions, especially in urban and semi-urban areas with inadequate sanitation and standing water, which serve as breeding grounds for mosquitoes.

In severe cases, it can lead to dengue hemorrhagic fever, characterized by bleeding, low platelet count, and organ damage. Dengue is prevalent in tropical and subtropical regions, especially in urban and semi-urban areas with inadequate sanitation and standing water, which serve as breeding grounds for mosquitoes.

To prevent dengue fever, it's essential to eliminate mosquito breeding sites, use mosquito repellents, wear protective clothing, and use mosquito nets. There is currently no specific treatment for dengue fever, but supportive care can help manage symptoms and prevent complications.

About Us
